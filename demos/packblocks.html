Packing blocks into a cube

One of the most well-known inequalities states that the arithmatic mean of a sequence of numbers is always greater or equal to its geometric mean.

This is a bit dry and hard to imagine when stated this way. It is possible to visualize this inequality in a tangible and more intuitive way.

Given 3 numbers a, b, c...

(a+b+c)/3 ≥ (abc)1/3
(a+b+c)3/27 ≥ abc
(a+b+c)3 ≥ 27abc

This means that we can fit 27 blocks of side length a, b and c into a cube of side length a+b+c.
